# Bridge Variants by Signal Type

Detailed bridge library for each signal type. Use these to vary openers across a campaign and prevent pattern-matching.

## Hiring Signal

Reference a posted role they are hiring for. Strongest when the role is growth, marketing, ops, or business development.

### Variant A bridges (15-20 words)

1. "With the [role] role you posted recently, thought this would be relevant as you scale [team/market/operations] further."
2. "Given the [role] opening you have posted, thought this might be of interest as you build out the team."
3. "Saw the [role] role you opened recently, figured this would be relevant given the growth direction you are heading."

### Variant B bridges (8-14 words)

1. "Saw the [role] role you posted [N] weeks back. Different angle worth flagging."
2. "Your [role] search is [N] weeks in. A few [vertical] operators went a different direction lately."
3. "Saw the [role] opening. The hire matters; what they walk into matters more."
4. "[Role] role still open. We help [vertical] cover that workload with AI agents."
5. "Saw the [role] role from [N] weeks back. Curious what the first 90-day workload looks like."

## Pain Signal (Public Frustration Post)

Reference a public post about a specific operational frustration. Highest authentic timing signal because the prospect named their own pain.

### Variant A bridges

1. "Saw your post about [topic] lately, thought this would be of interest given the direction you are heading."
2. "Your recent thinking on [topic] caught my attention, thought this would be relevant for compounding what is working."

### Variant B bridges

1. "Caught your [topic] post last week. Adjacent fix worth a quick look."
2. "Saw the [topic] thread you posted. Different framing on it might be useful."
3. "Your post on [topic] was sharp. Related thought worth sharing."

2. "Caught the question you posted on [forum] about [topic], figured this would be relevant for what you are building toward."

### Variant B bridges

2. "Caught your [topic] question on [forum]. Quick take worth sharing."

## Expansion / Acquisition Signal

Reference an announced market expansion or portfolio acquisition. Signals active capital deployment and growth focus.

### Variant A bridges

1. "With the [city/market] expansion you announced, thought this would be of interest as you build out the new market."
2. "Given the [acquisition/expansion] you posted about, thought this might be relevant as you integrate and grow the combined book."
3. "Saw the [market] expansion announcement, figured this would be relevant as you scale into the new region."

### Variant B bridges

1. "Saw the [city] expansion. Out-of-area customers there are mostly ungoverned."
2. "Caught the [acquisition] announcement. Integrating new accounts cleanly is the underrated part."
3. "Saw the [market] launch. Timing on this is interesting for you."

2. "Saw you attended [event] recently, figured this would be relevant given what you are clearly investing in."

2. "Your panel at [event] on [topic] was solid. Related thought worth sharing."
3. "Caught the [event] attendance. Three things worth flagging from that direction."

## Profile-Based Fallback (Tier 4 — use only when no signals available)

When no behavioral or activity signals are detectable, fall back to specific profile attributes. These are weaker but still functional when no stronger signal exists.

### Variant A bridges

1. "Given your deep [niche] expertise, thought this could be of interest for you to scale your [type] business even further."
2. "With [N]+ [units/customers/footprint] across [region], thought this might be relevant as you continue expanding."
3. "Your focus on [specialization] made me think this would be of interest as that market keeps heating up."
4. "Operating across [N] [states/markets] is no small feat, so thought this might be useful as you keep scaling."

### Variant B bridges

1. "[N]+ [units] in [region]. Different growth angle worth a look."
2. "Solo to [N] [units] in [years]. That is not a normal pace."
3. "[N] [markets/states] with one growth team. Curious how that works."

## Choosing the right bridge

When multiple signals are available, prioritize them by recency and specificity:

1. Most recent (within 7 days) wins over older signals
2. More specific signals (named topic, named role, named market) win over generic ones
3. Active intent signals (hiring, expansion, frustration posts) win over passive signals (likes, follows)

When generating multiple variants for A/B testing, vary across signal types AND across length variants. Do not vary just the wording of the same signal — that does not actually test different hypotheses.
