# Offer Library by Service Type

Reference library of proven free deliverables organized by service category. Use this as inspiration when generating offers, but always customize to the user's specific service and audience.

## AI / Automation Services

### Strong offers
- "Custom workflow blueprint mapping 3 of your manual processes with specific automation opportunities and projected time savings."
- "AI agent demo configured for one of your specific use cases (e.g., your inbound inquiries) with a 60-second response example."
- "Audit of your top 5 manual workflows with specific tools and AI agents that would replace each one."
- "Custom GPT/agent built for one repeated task in your business — yours to keep regardless."
- "Tech stack teardown identifying 3 redundant tools and projected monthly savings."

### Weak offers (avoid)
- "Free demo of our platform" — implies they need to learn software
- "Free AI strategy session" — meeting, not deliverable
- "Free guide to AI in [industry]" — gated content, generic

## Patterns across all categories

Looking at the strong offers in every category, these patterns hold:

1. **Always has a number.** "5 scripts," "200-prospect list," "10-page audit." Numbers create specificity and credibility.

2. **Names a deliverable, not an activity.** "Audit" alone is weak. "Audit of your top 10 pages with prioritized fixes" is strong because it names what they receive.

3. **References their specific situation.** "Built for your audience" or "specific to your market" or "matched to your portfolio" — personalization in the offer itself increases perceived value.

4. **Has a built-in time horizon.** "Ready to ship this week" or "useful next quarter" makes the value concrete and immediate.

5. **Includes a "useful regardless" framing.** "Yours to keep whether we work together or not" removes the perceived obligation to engage further.

The strongest offers combine 4-5 of these patterns. The weakest offers combine 0-1.
