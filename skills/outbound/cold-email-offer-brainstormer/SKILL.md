# Cold Email Offer Brainstormer

Brainstorm specific tangible free deliverables that turn cold email CTAs from "want to chat?" into "you would be crazy to say no." The offer is the most important variable in a cold email. This skill exists because most service founders are too close to their work to see what would actually be valuable to a stranger.

## When to use this skill

Trigger this skill when the user wants to:

- Design the CTA offer for a cold email campaign
- Brainstorm what to give away free in outreach
- Find the "first step" of their service to offer for free
- Improve a cold email that has a weak or generic CTA
- Convert a "free consultation" or "free audit" CTA into something stronger

This skill pairs with `cold-email-body-writer`. That skill writes the email body around an offer. This skill creates the offer itself.

## The core principle

A free deliverable in 2026 must satisfy four tests. If it fails any test, the campaign will underperform regardless of how well the rest of the email is written.

1. **Tangible.** A finished output the prospect can hold, view, or use. Not a meeting. Not a "discussion." Not a "discovery call."
2. **Specific.** Has a number ("5 scripts," "200-prospect list," "10-page audit"). Vague offers are pattern-matched as automated outreach.
3. **Valuable on its own.** The prospect could use it immediately, even if they never hire the user.
4. **Proves capability.** Doing it well demonstrates the user can deliver the paid version.

A "free 30-minute consultation" fails three of four tests. A "5-video script set built for your specific listings" passes all four. The difference is reply rate.

## When to start brainstorming

Before generating offer ideas, gather this information from the user. If they have not provided it, ask:

1. **What service do they sell?** (e.g., AI growth infrastructure, CRO consulting, video production)
2. **Who do they sell to?** (specific persona: PM company owners, DTC ecom founders, real estate agents)
3. **What outcome do they deliver?** (more owner clients, higher conversion rate, more inbound calls)
4. **What is the first step of their normal paid process?** (this is usually the strongest free offer)
5. **What is the price point of the paid service?** (determines how much the offer can cost to produce)
6. **What proof or experience do they have?** (helps shape what they can credibly offer)

If the user is impatient or wants ideas before answering all questions, generate based on what is available and note assumptions. Do not block the brainstorm waiting for perfect inputs.

## The brainstorm framework

Generate 8-12 distinct offers across these five categories. Variety matters — testing 3 offers from the same category does not actually test different hypotheses.

### Category 1: First Step of the Paid Process (Strongest)

The first step of the user's normal paid engagement, productized as a standalone free deliverable. This is the strongest offer category because it actually proves they can deliver the paid version.

Examples by service type:

- **AI/automation services:** First step is usually system audit or workflow blueprint. Free deliverable: "Custom workflow blueprint mapping 3 of your manual processes with specific automation opportunities."
- **Outbound services:** First step is target account list. Free deliverable: "Target list of 200 prospects matched to your ICP, with verified emails."
- **CRO services:** First step is a conversion audit. Free deliverable: "Audit of your top 5 product pages with specific layout fixes and predicted impact."
- **SEO services:** First step is technical and content audit. Free deliverable: "SEO audit of your top 10 pages with prioritized fixes you could ship next week."
- **Content services:** First step is content strategy. Free deliverable: "12-topic content roadmap built from competitor gaps and search demand data."

### Category 2: Diagnostic Output

A specific analysis of their current state delivered as a written document. Different from a generic "audit" because it produces an actionable artifact.

Examples:

- "Teardown of your top 3 competitors with positioning gaps and 5 differentiation angles."
- "Customer journey audit with 7 friction points identified across acquisition to retention."
- "Tech stack audit identifying 3 redundant tools and projected savings."
- "Conversion path analysis with bottleneck identified and predicted lift from fixing it."

### Category 3: Strategic Asset

A planning document or framework they could implement themselves. Strongest when it includes specific details about their business.

Examples:

- "90-day growth roadmap for [their specific situation]."
- "ICP definition document with 5 priority customer segments and messaging angles for each."
- "Pricing teardown with 3 tested pricing structures for your service."
- "Quarterly content calendar mapped to your sales cycle."

### Category 4: Production Asset

Something the prospect could deploy immediately. Highest perceived value because it requires the least translation.

Examples:

- "5 video scripts written for your business, ready to film this week."
- "3 ad creatives plus copy you could run on your own."
- "Landing page mockup for one of your top products."
- "10 LinkedIn posts written in your voice, ready to publish."
- "Email sequence (5 messages) for one of your priority customer segments."

### Category 5: Data Asset

A list, dataset, or research output that has standalone value.

Examples:

- "Target list of [N] prospects matched to your ICP with verified contacts."
- "Database of [N] [thought leaders/decision makers/etc.] in your market."
- "Compensation benchmarks for 3 roles you are hiring."
- "Pricing benchmarks across 20 competitors in your category."

## Selection criteria for the strongest offer

After generating 8-12 offers, score each on these five criteria (1-5 scale):

1. **Specificity:** Does it have a number? Is the deliverable named precisely?
2. **Standalone value:** Could they use it without hiring you?
3. **Capability proof:** Does delivering it well prove you can deliver the paid version?
4. **Production cost:** Can you produce it in 30-90 minutes per prospect (sustainable at scale)?
5. **Curiosity trigger:** Does the offer create a "wait, can I really get that for free?" moment?

The winning offer scores 4-5 on all five. If an offer scores 3 or below on any criterion, replace it.

### Production cost rule

Free deliverables that take 30-90 minutes per prospect are sustainable. Anything taking longer (full-day audits, custom builds, multi-week projects) breaks the unit economics if you reach 50+ prospects.

The exception: if the deliverable can be 80% AI-generated (using Claude, custom GPTs, or workflows) with 20% human review, longer-feeling outputs are sustainable. Example: a "12-topic content roadmap" might look like 4 hours of work but can be generated in 20 minutes via AI with human verification.

## Output format

When brainstorming offers, produce them in this format:

```
## Offer Brainstorm for [USER'S SERVICE] targeting [USER'S AUDIENCE]

### The strongest offers (top 3)

1. [Offer name — specific deliverable with number]
   - What it is: [1-2 sentence description]
   - Why it works: [scoring on the 5 criteria]
   - Production effort: [time estimate]
   - Sample CTA wording: "I would love to [verb] [offer] for [{{company}} or "your business"] — completely free."

2. [Second strongest offer]
   ...

3. [Third strongest offer]
   ...

### Additional viable offers (4-6 more, briefer descriptions)

- [Offer 4 + 1-line description]
- [Offer 5 + 1-line description]
- ...

### Offers to AVOID for this service

- [Generic offer that would fail] — why it fails
- [Generic offer that would fail] — why it fails
```

Always end with a recommendation on which offer to test first and why.

## Worked example

### User input
- Service: AI growth infrastructure for property management companies
- Audience: PM company owners with 100-1000 doors, US-based
- Outcome delivered: 50-150 new doors added in 12 months
- First step of paid process: ICP definition + initial landlord list build
- Price point: $7,500 setup + $5,500/month
- Proof: 100+ service businesses in prior agency, no PM-specific case studies yet

### Output

```
## Offer Brainstorm for AI Growth Infrastructure targeting Property Management Owners

### The strongest offers (top 3)

1. Target List of 200 Absentee Landlords Matched to Your Portfolio
   - What it is: A spreadsheet of 200 absentee landlords in their market who own 3+ rental properties, filtered by criteria like out-of-state ownership, recent eviction filings, and equity position. Includes verified email and LinkedIn match where available.
   - Why it works:
     - Specificity: 5/5 (specific number, specific filters, specific data)
     - Standalone value: 5/5 (they could outreach to this list themselves)
     - Capability proof: 5/5 (this is literally the first step of the paid system)
     - Production cost: 4/5 (30-45 min per prospect using PropStream + Clay + AI scoring)
     - Curiosity trigger: 5/5 ("they will give me 200 leads for free?")
   - Production effort: 45 minutes per prospect, 80% automatable
   - Sample CTA wording: "I would love to build a free target list of 200 absentee landlords matched to your portfolio profile — useful whether we work together or not."

2. Speed-to-Lead Audit with 60-Second Response Demo
   - What it is: Submits a test inquiry through their existing channels (website, Zillow listing, etc.), tracks response time, and delivers a written audit comparing them to top performers in their market. Includes a recorded demo of an AI receptionist responding in 60 seconds to the same inquiry.
   - Why it works:
     - Specificity: 5/5 (specific test, specific output)
     - Standalone value: 4/5 (the audit itself is useful even without the demo)
     - Capability proof: 5/5 (demonstrates the exact system you would build)
     - Production cost: 3/5 (60-90 minutes per prospect)
     - Curiosity trigger: 5/5 ("they tested my response time?")
   - Production effort: 75 minutes per prospect
   - Sample CTA wording: "I would love to test your current inquiry response process and show you a 60-second AI alternative — free, takes 10 minutes of your time to review."

3. Owner Acquisition Playbook with 5 Channel Breakdowns
   - What it is: A 5-page document mapping out 5 owner acquisition channels (paid ads, public records outbound, referral systems, SEO, partnerships) with specific tactics, costs, and expected ROI for their market and door count.
   - Why it works:
     - Specificity: 4/5 (specific number of channels, specific format)
     - Standalone value: 5/5 (they could execute on this themselves)
     - Capability proof: 4/5 (proves strategic depth, less directly than the list)
     - Production cost: 5/5 (highly templatable, 30 min per prospect with AI assist)
     - Curiosity trigger: 4/5 (lower than the list because "playbook" sounds generic)
   - Production effort: 30 minutes per prospect, fully templatable
   - Sample CTA wording: "I would love to put together a free 5-channel owner acquisition playbook customized to your market and door count."

### Additional viable offers

- AI Receptionist demo configured for their specific business (90-min build, very compelling but harder to scale)
- Competitive teardown of 3 local PM companies with positioning gaps (40 min, useful but less actionable than the list)
- 30-day quick-win plan to add 10 doors using existing audience (35 min, good for smaller PMs)
- ICP definition document with 5 owner profiles for their market (45 min, useful but less differentiated)
- Speed-to-lead audit only (without the AI demo) (45 min, good but weaker than #2 above)
- Custom GPT trained on their FAQ for owner inquiries (60 min, very compelling for tech-curious PMs)

### Offers to AVOID for this service

- "Free 30-minute strategy call" — pattern-matched as discovery call. Reply rate will be terrible.
- "Free PM growth audit" — too vague. "Audit" without a specific output is read as automated.
- "Free guide on AI in property management" — gated content. Recipients have downloaded 50 of these. No value.
- "Free trial of our system" — implies they have to learn software. Wrong promise for a done-for-you service.

### Recommendation

Test Offer #1 (Target List of 200 Absentee Landlords) first. Highest scores across all five criteria, lowest production cost per prospect, and most directly proves the paid system value. This is also the offer that scales best — you can use the same data infrastructure that powers your paid service to produce these lists at near-zero marginal cost after the first 5 prospects.

If you have capacity to test 2 offers in parallel, run Offer #1 against Offer #2 (Speed-to-Lead Audit). They appeal to different motivations: #1 to growth ambition, #2 to operational anxiety. Reply rates will tell you which motivation is stronger in your market.
```

## Common failure patterns to avoid

When brainstorming offers, watch for these failures:

1. **The "free consultation" trap.** Even disguised as "free strategy session" or "free 15-minute audit call," a meeting is not a deliverable. Replace with a finished output.

2. **The "everything we do" offer.** "Free full audit of your entire business" sounds generous but is too big to take seriously and too expensive to deliver. Narrow it.

3. **The unverifiable offer.** "Free analysis of why you are losing customers" requires data the user does not have. Stick to offers that use publicly available data or data the prospect can easily share.

4. **The PDF download offer.** "Download our free guide" is a gated lead magnet, not a personalized deliverable. Recipients distinguish these instantly.

5. **The "lite version" trap.** Offering a worse version of the paid service trains prospects to expect a worse experience. Offer something different in kind, not lesser in quality.

6. **The non-proving offer.** A offer is wasted if doing it well does not prove you can do the paid version. "Free social media tips" does not prove anything if you sell ad services.

## Quality checklist

Before presenting offers, verify each one:

- [ ] Is a specific deliverable (not a meeting)
- [ ] Has a number in its description
- [ ] Could be used standalone by the prospect
- [ ] Doing it well demonstrates capability
- [ ] Takes 30-90 minutes per prospect to produce
- [ ] Sounds curiosity-triggering, not transactional
- [ ] Differentiated from generic agency offers
- [ ] Can be produced before the discovery call (so they have it whether they engage or not)
