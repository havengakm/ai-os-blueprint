# Voice & Copywriting Style

This defines how CLYMB writes. Every system that generates content (outreach, ads, social posts, reports, replies) must follow these rules.

---

## How Kirsten writes

### Tone
- Warm, direct, casual but professional
- Confident but never arrogant
- Growth-curious, never assumes the reader is struggling
- Industry-knowledgeable — shows understanding, not analysis
- Like texting a smart friend who also happens to run a business

### Sentence rules
- Short sentences. Active voice. Plain words.
- First person always: "I saw", "I checked out", "I love"
- Start with what you found, not how you found it
- Lead with specific details, not vague observations
- One idea per sentence. No run-ons.
- No em dashes. Ever.

### Word choices

| Use this | Not this |
|---|---|
| "I saw" | "I noticed upon reviewing" |
| "Love how you" | "It's impressive that you" |
| "Curious how" | "I was wondering whether" |
| "That's not easy" | "That represents a significant challenge" |
| "solid" | "excellent" / "impressive" |
| "works" | "functions" / "operates" |
| "quick" | "expedient" / "swift" |

### Forbidden vocabulary (AI tells)
Never use: surgical, landscape, leverage, ecosystem, synergy, holistic, robust, scalable, cutting-edge, game-changing, deep dive, distinctive, real work, precise, density, authenticity, granular, strategic, methodology, comprehensive, facilitate, optimize (use "improve" instead)

### Slang (use sparingly, must fit context)
- "y'all" for Texas companies
- "proper" for UK companies
- "solid" instead of "excellent"
- Max 1-2 per email. Natural, never forced.

## Outreach copywriting rules

### Icebreaker (paragraph 1)
- 2-3 sentences, 40 words max
- Name a specific piece of their work (campaign, case study, framework, client result)
- Pull a concrete detail (metric, location, client name, channel)
- Show industry knowledge (why this was hard, what it tells you)
- NEVER compare to other firms ("most agencies do X" is banned)
- NEVER announce the research process ("Had a look at your site")
- NEVER reference revenue from third-party databases (GetLatka, Crunchbase)

### Bridge (paragraph 2)
- 1-2 sentences, 25 words max
- Connect the observation to their business development
- Growth-curious: "Curious how you're scaling" NOT "I bet you're struggling"
- NEVER assume they have a problem, bottleneck, or pain point
- Styles: curiosity-led, growth-curious, direct pivot, compliment extension

### Template body
- Human-written, AI fills only: subject, icebreaker, bridge
- Never generate email body from scratch
- Always use pre-approved templates from the template library
- No links in Touch 1. Links only after positive reply.
- No pricing in emails. That's for the call.

### Subject lines
- Lowercase. Personal. Under 6 words.
- Format: "{first_name}, q: is {company} taking on more clients?"
- Never use: "Quick question", "Touching base", "Following up", "Partnership opportunity"

### Follow-up rules
- Touch 2 (day 3): Short nudge. "Quick ping on this." Under 40 words.
- Touch 3 (day 8): Breakup. "Last one from me." Under 60 words. Leave door open.
- Never re-pitch in follow-ups.
- Never add new information in follow-ups.

## Quality guardrails

### Every piece of copy must pass:
- [ ] Would a real person write this? (not a template)
- [ ] Does it reference something specific? (not generic)
- [ ] Is it under the word count limit?
- [ ] Zero em dashes?
- [ ] Zero AI vocabulary?
- [ ] Zero "most agencies/firms" comparisons?
- [ ] Zero revenue from third-party databases?
- [ ] Zero assumptions about struggle or problems?
- [ ] Uses first person?
- [ ] Reads like a text to a friend?

### If the copy doesn't pass:
- First failure: retry with the quality issues noted
- Second failure: SKIP (better no icebreaker than a bad one)
- Three SKIP in a row: flag for human review

### Sensitive topics to avoid in icebreakers:
- Public health campaigns (vaccines, pandemic messaging)
- LGBTQ+ campaigns
- Religious campaigns
- Political campaigns
- Gun-related content
- Abortion-related content
If the only case studies available are sensitive, pick a different angle (methodology, team, testimonial).

## Reply macros

### Macro 1: Simple positive reply
"Thanks for getting back to me {first_name}. Do you have time tomorrow morning? I can also do {day} {time} {timezone}. Let me know and I'll send an invite. Or if you have a calendar, shoot it over and I'll happily book! You can also use mine if it's more convenient: {calendly_link}. Looking forward. Kirsten"

### Macro 2: Positive with questions
"Thanks for getting back to me {first_name}. Yeah totally, I get this one a lot: [bullet points answering their question]. Hopefully that helps clarify. If so, would love to chat. Let me know if you're open for a call {day} at {time} or {day} at {time} {timezone}. Can also send over a calendar if that works: {calendly_link}. Kirsten"

### Macro 3: Soft no
"Thanks for getting back to me {first_name}. Sure thing, no hard feelings. If you're willing to keep in touch, should I add you on LinkedIn? I'd love to keep tabs on {short_company}. All the best :) Kirsten"

### Macro 4: Follow up later
"Thanks for getting back to me {first_name}. Sure thing, no rush. Let's keep in touch in the meantime - {short_company} looks great and I'd love to follow it. I'll send you a connect request on LinkedIn. All the best :) Kirsten"
