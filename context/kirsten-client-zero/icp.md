# Ideal Client Profile: Client Zero (Kirsten — AIOS deployment)

Ported from `base-camp-agents/.claude/skills/kirsten-icp/SKILL.md` with one narrowing: Client Zero's first outbound push targets **Creative / Branding agencies** specifically (Avatar 1, Tier 1). Avatars 2 and 3 stay in this document for later expansion but are out of scope for the first send.

## Primary ICP

Digital B2B service business founders. 35-55 years old. English-speaking markets. They have clients, cash flow, and a real pipeline bottleneck. Revenue depends on them personally.

**Four segments in priority order (Client Zero runs Tier 1 first):**

1. **Specialist digital and creative agencies** (branding, UX/UI, CRO, SEO, PR, influencer, web dev, software dev, architectural/interior design)
2. Fractional executives and B2B consultants (fractional CMO/COO/CFO/CTO, change management, growth advisors, revenue ops, B2B sales trainers, executive coaches serving businesses)
3. Recruiting and staffing agencies
4. B2B tech and high-touch SaaS

**Revenue:** $300K–$2M (SaaS up to $5M). Team: 1–50 people. Geographies: US, UK, Canada, Australia, NZ, South Africa.

**The shared problem across all segments:** Business development depends entirely on the founder. When they're busy, pipeline dries up. They know they need a system. They haven't built one.

### What They've Tried That Failed

- Marketing agencies (poor leads, pipeline dies when they leave, they own nothing)
- DIY tools (overwhelmed, gave up, tools still billing)
- BD hires (no infrastructure to support them, set up to fail)
- Referrals and hope (no visibility, no predictability, no control)
- Content and LinkedIn (inconsistent, slow, unclear ROI)

### Buying Triggers

- Lost a client and felt the fragility
- Quiet quarter after a busy one
- Tried an agency or tool and it didn't work
- Competitor growing faster with a smaller team
- Considering hiring a BD person but can't justify the cost or risk
- Spouse asking why they're always working

## Avatar 1: The Agency Founder (Tier 1 — Client Zero primary target)

Founder or co-founder of a specialist digital/creative agency. 5–15 years in business. Built the agency on the back of their expertise and reputation. Team of 3–20 people. Revenue $300K–$2M. Good at what they do. The problem is pipeline.

### Their daily reality

They open their laptop. Check email for new enquiries. There aren't any. Check LinkedIn. Nothing interesting. Open their project management tool: three client projects need attention, a team member has a question, a deliverable is late. They spend the morning putting out fires and doing client work.

Around 2pm they think "I should really do some business development." They open LinkedIn. Scroll. Maybe draft a post. Delete it. Feel guilty. Go back to client work.

At 6pm: two existing clients, one might be wrapping up, no new conversations, a referral from three weeks ago that went nowhere.

They close the laptop thinking "I need to fix this" for the 50th time this quarter.

**Week breakdown:**
- 60–70% client delivery and team management
- 15–20% admin, invoicing, operations
- 10–15% thinking about BD but not actually doing it
- 0–5% actual outbound prospecting

### Their struggles

**The pipeline problem:** Revenue is unpredictable. No system for generating new business. Depends entirely on referrals, past clients coming back, occasional LinkedIn posts, warm introductions. They know they should do outbound. They've tried briefly: 20 emails, no replies, gave up.

**The founder-as-BD problem:** They are the BD team. When they're delivering, BD stops. When BD stops, pipeline dries up 6–8 weeks later. Classic feast-or-famine. They've considered hiring a BD person. The maths is terrifying. $4K–$8K/month for someone who takes 3–6 months to ramp and might leave in a year.

**The "too busy to grow" trap:** When delivering, can't sell. When selling, can't deliver. Growth stalls. They can never do both simultaneously.

**The margin problem (the silent killer):** Most agencies run below 20% margin. On $100K/month revenue: $20K profit after payroll, tools, contractors. They feel busy and successful. Underneath, the profit doesn't match the effort. A small margin improvement has outsized impact: moving from 20% to 25% on $100K revenue is $5K extra/month, $60K extra/year.

**What they've already tried:**
- Sent 20 emails once. No replies. Stopped.
- Ran ads. Spent $2K–$5K/month on Google or LinkedIn. Wrong enquiries. Poor ROI. Stopped.
- Posted on LinkedIn for 3 months. 200 likes. Zero new clients.
- Hired a BD person. Failed, or did the maths and decided they couldn't afford the risk.

### Their fears

- "What if this dry spell lasts another 3 months?"
- "What if my best client leaves and I have nothing to replace them?"
- "What if I have to let team members go?"
- "What if outbound damages my reputation? I don't want to be seen as spammy."
- "What if I spend money on another experiment that doesn't work?"
- "What if I'm just not good at sales?"

The deepest fear: they built something good but can't grow it, and they don't know why.

### Their dream outcome

**Immediate:** 3–5 qualified meetings per month with prospects they'd actually want to work with. Pipeline visible enough to forecast next month. BD happening without them personally pushing it every day.

**Lifestyle:** Stop being the growth bottleneck. Spend time on strategy and creative direction. Take a holiday without the pipeline dying. Hire with confidence. Say no to bad-fit clients because they have options. Feel like a CEO, not a freelancer with employees.

**In their own words:**
- "I just want to wake up on Monday and know that new business is happening without me pushing it."
- "I want to focus on the work, not constantly worry about where the next client is coming from."
- "I want to have options. Right now I take every project that comes in because I'm scared of the gap."

### Their objections

**"I've tried outbound before and it didn't work."** They sent 20–50 emails manually. Generic copy. No system. No follow-up. They're comparing a system to a half-hearted attempt.

**"I don't want to be seen as spammy."** Their reputation matters. They've received terrible cold emails themselves. They need to know the outreach will sound like them, be genuinely personalised, and not damage their brand.

**"I don't have time to manage another system."** This is the whole point. But they've been burned by tools that promised automation and created more work.

**"I can't afford to experiment right now."** Cash flow is tight. Trust is low. They need the risk on Kirsten, not on them.

**"My business is different."** Every agency owner thinks their situation is unique. The pipeline problem is universal. The specifics vary, the pattern doesn't.

### What they value most (in order)

1. Predictability: knowing where next month's revenue is coming from
2. Quality: right-fit prospects, not random enquiries
3. Autonomy: it works without them babysitting it
4. Reputation safety: it won't damage their brand
5. Proof: show me it works for someone like me
6. Low risk: don't make me bet the farm
7. Speed: I needed this yesterday

### Before and after

| | Before | After |
|---|---|---|
| Pipeline | Depends on founder's personal effort | 3–5 qualified meetings/month without founder involvement |
| Revenue | Unpredictable month to month | Visible and forecastable |
| BD time | 0–5% (on a good week) | Runs daily in the background |
| New business source | Referrals only | Referrals + systematic outbound |
| Client choice | Takes everything (can't afford gaps) | Can say no to bad-fit clients |
| BD hire | Terrifying cost on thin margins | Not needed |
| Margin | Below 20% | Improving toward 25–30% |
| Feels like | Freelancer with employees | CEO of a real business |

## Universal Objections

**"I've tried this before."** Every approach you've tried had a structural flaw. This is different because we build it together, you own it, and it keeps running after we're done.

**"I'm not technical."** You don't need to be. Kirsten handles the technology. You handle the business context. Every client says the same thing after week 1: that was way less complicated than I expected.

**"I don't have time."** You're spending time worrying about pipeline right now. This replaces worry with a system.

**"I can't justify the cost."** If this lands you one extra client per quarter you wouldn't have had otherwise, it's paid for the entire year. Everything after that is profit.

**"How do I know it works for my industry?"** Every service business has the same pipeline problem. The industry changes the icebreaker. The problem is identical.
