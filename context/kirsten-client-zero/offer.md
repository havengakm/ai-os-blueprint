## What Kirsten Actually Sells (Right Now — Client Zero First Few)

**Product:** The AI Operating System (AIOS) foundation + the Scout outbound system deployed on top.

**Price shape (first few clients):**
- **$2,500 one-time setup fee.** Non-refundable. Covers ICP definition, voice calibration, infrastructure (domains, warmed inboxes, Trigify/Smartlead configuration), the 14-variant component library tuned to their niche, first 30 days of active tuning.
- **Outcome-based retainer (kicks in after the guarantee):** $2,500/month for continued operation + optimisation. Starts only after hitting **10 qualified meetings with their ideal prospect in the first 60 days** (90 days for small markets). If the milestone isn't met, no retainer starts until it is.

**What they own (all of it):**
All prospect data, outreach copy, domains, sending accounts, Trigify configuration, Smartlead account, performance history, dashboard access. Exportable at any time. No lock-in.

## The Guarantee

**10 qualified meetings within 60 days** of go-live. The retainer does not start a day before that threshold is met. If it isn't met:
- (a) Free 30-day extension — system keeps running, fresh messaging, adjusted targeting.
- (b) Walk away — no further obligation, client keeps all infrastructure.

"Qualified" is defined together at onboarding: industry, company size, decision-maker level, budget fit. If a meeting doesn't match the agreed criteria, it doesn't count.

## One-liner (for conversation, never for email)

"I build AI outbound systems that belong to your business. You own the system. I power the engine."

**Salesforce analogy:** "Think of it like Salesforce. You own your CRM data, your pipeline, your reports. Salesforce provides the platform. Your outbound system works the same way — your data, your voice, your prospects, your meetings. All yours. The setup fee covers the build. The monthly fee covers the AI, the agents, and the infrastructure keeping it alive."

## What the System Does

- Finds ideal clients matching the client's ICP with active buying signals (via Trigify + structured research)
- Reaches out in their voice — personalised per-contact, not templated
- Handles follow-ups across multiple channels
- Books qualified meetings directly into their calendar
- Client shows up to the meeting. Everything before that is handled.

## Client Obligations (guarantee contingent on these)

1. Complete onboarding within 10 business days of signing
2. Respond to approval requests within 48 hours
3. Do not change ICP mid-campaign (minor adjustments okay; fundamental changes reset the guarantee period)
4. Make themselves available for booked meetings
5. Do not modify the system or infrastructure without approval
6. Maintain the retainer on schedule (once it has started)

## ROI Math (use on sales calls)

**Revenue angle:** Average agency project $15–50K. System books 3–5 meetings/month at 20% close rate = 1 new client/month. System cost (setup + retainer): a fraction of that. ROI: 5–25x.

**Margin angle:** Typical agency margin below 20%. No BD hire saves $4–8K/month. Better client selectivity improves margin toward 25–30%.

**Replacement cost:** BD hire $4–8K/month + 3–6 month ramp + churn risk. Lead gen agency $3–10K/month + no ownership. LinkedIn ads $2–5K/month + wrong prospects. This system: fraction of the cost, 24/7, client keeps the infrastructure.

**The one-liner for sales calls:** "If this lands you one extra client per quarter you wouldn't have had otherwise, it's paid for the entire year. Everything after that is profit."

## Never Say About the Offer

- "Guaranteed results" — say "10 qualified meetings in 60 days or the retainer doesn't start"
- "Revolutionary", "game-changing", "cutting-edge"
- Price in the first email (let them ask on the call)
- "Retainer" in cold email — in conversation fine; in email copy use placeholder phrasing
- "We'll manage your campaigns" — say "the system handles it"
- "Our team will..." — say "I" or "the system"
- "AI agent", "chatbot", "bot" — say "AI team member" or "the system"
- Compare to named competitors in writing
- Offer discounts — price resistance means wrong prospect, not wrong price
