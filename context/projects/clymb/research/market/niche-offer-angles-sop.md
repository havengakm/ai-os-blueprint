# Niche Offer Angles SOP

Research-backed offer positioning per niche. Based on Reddit r/agency research (April 2026) and web research.

---

## Core Insight

Cold outreach is already the #1 client acquisition channel for agencies (31% of 392 surveyed). They're doing it manually, inconsistently, and badly. The offer is NOT "try cold email." The offer is: **"The cold outreach you're already doing, but automated and personalised so it actually works without you doing it."**

One-liner per niche. The email books the call, the call closes the deal. No pricing, no setup details, no specifics beyond this:

**Shopify/eCommerce:**
"I'll put 10 qualified DTC brand owners on your calendar in the next 60 days. Brands actively running ads, real teams that need ecom growth partners. I work primarily on a results-first approach."

**Creative/Branding:**

## Offer Positioning Per Niche

### Niche 1: Full-stack Shopify/eCommerce Agencies

**Their world:**
- Build and optimise Shopify stores for DTC brands
- $10-50K projects, $3-10K/mo retainers
- Client quality matters more than quantity (a $5M supplement brand > a $200K supplement brand)
- Competing against thousands of Shopify agencies

**Offer angle:** "Find Shopify stores doing $1M+ that are ready for a full-stack partner. Not tyre-kickers from the partner directory."

**Template variables:**
- `{niche}`: eCommerce agencies
- `{niche_specific_term}`: DTC brands / Shopify store owners
- `{meetings_niche_term}`: discovery calls

**Pain-aware bridge examples:**
- "Curious how you're finding your next DTC brand right now."
- "I'm curious how much of your pipeline is still Shopify Partner directory vs outbound."

### Niche 2: CRO/Growth Marketing Agencies

**Their world:**
- Optimise funnels, landing pages, A/B tests for brands spending on ads
- $5-15K/mo retainers, $4K-35K/mo range
- Data-driven, understand ROI
- Clients churn after initial conversion lift

**Offer angle:** "Find brands spending $50K+/mo on ads who haven't optimised their funnel yet. They need you but don't know it."

**Template variables:**
- `{niche}`: growth marketing agencies
- `{niche_specific_term}`: brands / DTC companies
- `{meetings_niche_term}`: strategy calls

**Pain-aware bridge examples:**

### Niche 3: Creative/Branding Agencies

**Their world:**
- High-ticket projects ($15-50K+)
- Long sales cycles, relationship-driven
- Founded by creatives, not salespeople
- "A global glut of undifferentiated design firms means buyers have all the power"
- 79% over-service clients (scope creep eats margins)

**Offer angle:** "You're great at the work. We make sure the right people know you exist. Without you having to become a salesperson."

**Template variables:**
- `{niche}`: creative agencies
- `{niche_specific_term}`: brands / companies
- `{meetings_niche_term}`: conversations

**Pain-aware bridge examples:**
- "Curious how you're filling the pipeline between projects right now."
- "I'm curious how much of your new business still comes through who-you-know."

## Key Copy Rules (from research)

1. **Don't introduce cold email as a concept.** They already know it. 31% are doing it. Position as: "the outreach you're already doing, but it runs itself."

2. **Don't assume they're struggling.** Many are doing well. Be growth-curious: "curious how you're scaling" not "I bet you're struggling."

3. **Lead with their work, not their problem.** The icebreaker references their portfolio/case studies. The bridge pivots to pipeline. The template makes the offer.

4. **"Consistent" is the magic word.** "Finding consistent, quality leads without burning out" had 41 upvotes. Consistency > volume in their mind.

5. **"Without burning out" matters.** They're already working hard. The offer removes work, it doesn't add a new thing to manage.

6. **"Runs on autopilot" resonates.** They want a system that works while they deliver. Not another tool to learn.

7. **Performance/risk-reversal is expected.** "I work mostly on performance" and "results-first basis" are standard in this space. Include it but don't lead with it.

## Friday Review Cadence (Nick Saraev framework)

Weekly:
- Review reply rates per campaign
- Kill any campaign with <1% reply rate after 200+ sends
- Rewrite copy for campaigns between 1-3%
- Scale campaigns above 3% (increase sending volume, duplicate with variations)
- Add fresh leads every 2 weeks to prevent list fatigue

Monthly:
- Full funnel analysis: emails sent to revenue closed
- Calculate actual CAC per niche
- Cut underperforming niches, double down on winners
