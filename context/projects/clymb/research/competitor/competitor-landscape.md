## Direct Competitors (sell outbound systems to agencies)

| Competitor | What they do | Pricing | Strength | Weakness |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **LeftClick (Nick Saraev)** | Cold email systems for agencies. Maker School community. | $2.5K+ setup | Strong brand, proven methodology, large community | Perceived personalisation (not actual), teaching model |
| **Belkins** | B2B lead gen agency. SDR-as-a-service. | $3-5K/mo | Established, large team, proven results | Generic, agency model (they own the process) |
| **CIENCE** | SDR-as-a-service + data | $5-8K/mo | Enterprise-ready, comprehensive | Expensive, not founder-friendly |
| **Martal Group** | Lead gen for B2B tech | $3-5K/mo | Tech-focused, good data | Narrow niche, generic outreach |
| **SalesRoads** | Outsourced SDR team | $5K+/mo | Human SDRs, phone-focused | Expensive, not AI-powered |

## Our Differentiation

| What they all do | What CLYMB does differently |
|---|---|
| Pull from Apollo (same list everyone has) | Custom scraping (Clutch, DesignRush, Sortlist, ad libraries) |
| Send generic templates | Every email personalised with actual case study references |
| "Perceived personalisation" (vague references) | Real personalisation (specific campaigns, metrics, named clients) |
| One-size-fits-all copy | Avatar-matched frameworks (AIDA for growth-mode, Empathy for burned buyers) |
| Blast and pray | Signal-based timing (Trigify, hiring signals, Reddit activity) |
| They own the process | Client owns everything. Every account in their name. |
| Lock-in contracts | Month to month. Handover available. |
| Static system | Decision engine that gets smarter over time |
