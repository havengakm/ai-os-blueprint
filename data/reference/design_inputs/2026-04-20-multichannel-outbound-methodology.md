## What the video demonstrates

1. **Lead CSV with first name, last name, company name, company website, personal email, LinkedIn URL, company phone.** Company website and LinkedIn URL are mandatory because they are the research substrate.
2. **Per-lead AI personalization fields** generated by scraping the lead's LinkedIn profile and company website:
   - `icebreaker`: one sentence tailored compliment or observation, example "Love what you're doing at {company}."
   - `pain_point`: one sentence specific pain the company plausibly has, derived from the scrape.
3. **Conditional sequence:**
   - Day 0: LinkedIn connection request, no note.
   - Wait 3 days.
   - If accepted: LinkedIn message 1 containing `{first_name}`, `{icebreaker}`, `{pain_point}`, CTA.
   - If connection accepted and no reply after 1 day: follow-up email referencing the LinkedIn note.
   - If still no reply after 2 days: LinkedIn message 2 (short follow-up).
   - If connection NOT accepted after 3 days: two emails on the email-only path.
4. **AI autoresponder** handles all replies, optimized for "book a meeting" (sends Calendly link). Knowledge base PDF provides context for objection answers.
5. **Webhook fires on positive reply** to a Make.com scenario: sleep 60 seconds, then call Vapi API to place an outbound voice call to the lead, passing `first_name` and `phone_number` as dynamic variables into the Vapi assistant's prompt.
6. **Vapi assistant script** thanks the lead, confirms phone number, and texts a Calendly link so the lead can self-book.

## Vendor questions to resolve before the Voice plan

Kirsten's concern: Vapi may not be sophisticated enough for a high-ticket sale. A thank-you-and-text-a-link call is fine; an objection-handling qualification call is a different product.

Research questions to answer before the Voice plan is written:

1. **Conversational sophistication under objection.** Does the voice agent hold the thread when the prospect asks pricing, timelines, competitor comparisons, or raises a concrete objection? Vapi's default behaviour versus Dan Martell's product (Clay / his agency's branded agent, name to confirm) versus others (Bland, Retell, Synthflow, ElevenLabs Conversational AI).
2. **Naturalness of voice + latency.** Prospect drop-off if the agent sounds robotic or the turn latency is above ~500ms. Need to A/B sample real calls from each vendor.
3. **Prompt + knowledge-base ergonomics.** How objections and offer facts get loaded. Must match `feedback_copy_architecture` (human-written templates + AI placeholder fills), not free-form LLM generation.
4. **Call recording + transcript pipeline.** Transcripts must land in decision_log for QA review and weekly improvement cycle.
5. **Per-minute cost against `feedback_cost_management` caps.** Voice is the highest-cost channel by an order of magnitude. Budget envelope and auto-pause at 100% of tier cap applies here too.
6. **Compliance.** TCPA, state consent laws, DNC scrub, SMS consent basis. Phone/SMS SOP already drafted; voice adds recording-consent disclosure at call open.
7. **Failure fallback.** Call fails to connect or prospect hangs up fast. Does the system fall back to SMS with the Calendly link, or email, or human operator?

Use this checklist when writing the Voice plan's vendor-decision record, pattern on `2026-04-20-lead-sourcing-architecture.md`.
