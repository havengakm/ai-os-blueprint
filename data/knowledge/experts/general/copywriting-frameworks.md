# Copywriting Frameworks

Reference frameworks for cold outreach, ads, landing pages, and content. The AI OS selects the right framework based on the avatar, signal, and context.

---

## AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

**Best for:** Growth-mode founders, tech-curious buyers. People who respond to logic and momentum.

```
Attention: Hook with something specific they'll recognise
Interest: Connect it to a problem or opportunity they care about
Desire: Show what the solution looks like (outcome, not features)
Action: Clear, low-friction next step
```

**Cold email example:**
"I saw the Meow Mix case study. 8.3M reach on pet food. [Attention]
That kind of execution is why I'm curious how you're filling the pipeline on the new business side. [Interest]
I build outbound systems that put qualified brand owners on your calendar. Runs on autopilot. [Desire]
Can I send over a 90-sec video showing how this would work for Markerly? [Action]"

---

## PAS — Problem, Agitation, Solution

**Best for:** Burned agency buyers, overwhelmed founders. People in pain who need to feel understood before they'll listen.

**Important:** Never assume the problem. Use only when you have a confirmed signal (Reddit post, hiring signal, LinkedIn complaint). Growth-mode founders hate being told they have a problem.

```
Problem: Name the specific pain (from their signal, not assumed)
Agitation: Show you understand how it feels (not "imagine how bad it is")
Solution: Present the fix, tied to their specific situation
```

**Cold email example:**
"Saw your post about spending 10+ hours a week on prospecting between client calls. [Problem]
That's the trap. Delivery picks up, outreach stops. Then a project ends and it's a scramble. [Agitation]
I build systems that keep the pipeline running while you deliver. [Solution]"

**Warning:** Do NOT use PAS if you don't have a real signal. A PAS email without a real problem reads as presumptuous.

## BAB — Before, After, Bridge

**Best for:** Agency founders who are doing okay but want better. Shows transformation without implying they're failing.

```
Before: Where they are now (acknowledge it's working)
After: Where they could be (paint the picture)
Bridge: How to get there (your system)
```

**Cold email example:**
"Right now your best clients probably come through referrals and your network. It works. [Before]
Imagine if 10 qualified brand owners showed up on your calendar every month without you lifting a finger. [After]
That's what I build. An outbound system that runs in your voice, on autopilot. [Bridge]"

---

## Star, Story, Solution

**Best for:** Creative agencies, branding agencies, anyone who appreciates narrative. Also good for longer-form content and case studies.

```
Star: Introduce the hero (the prospect or someone like them)
Story: What happened (the journey, the struggle, the turning point)
Solution: How it resolved (your system as the resolution)
```

**Cold email example:**
"Ran my own agency for a decade. 100+ clients across 10 countries. Great at the work. Terrible at keeping the pipeline full. [Star + Story]
Built an AI system that does the prospecting for me. Could have been sipping cocktails in Bali a lot sooner. [Solution]
Now I install the same system for agencies like yours."

## SAS — Signal, Acknowledge, Segue

**Best for:** Signal-based outreach. When you have a Trigify social signal, hiring signal, or buying signal. This is the PRIMARY framework for CLYMB because signals beat generic observations.

```
Signal: Reference the specific signal you detected (their Reddit post, LinkedIn article, job listing)
Acknowledge: Show you understand what it means (not analysis, just awareness)
Segue: Naturally connect to why you're reaching out
```

**Cold email example:**
"I saw your Reddit post about hiring a Head of Sales. [Signal]
Usually means the pipeline is ready for more volume than one person can handle. [Acknowledge]
I build systems that fill the top of funnel so your new hire can focus on closing. [Segue]"

**Signal priority:** Trigify social > hiring revenue roles > org change > funding > website observation

## The Question Framework

**Best for:** Short, curiosity-driven emails. Touch 1 when you want to test a new angle. Subject line IS the hook.

```
One question that makes them think.
One sentence of context.
One line of credibility.
```

**Cold email example:**
Subject: "{first_name}, q: how much of {company}'s new business is still referral-driven?"

"Just curious. I work with agencies your size and most are 80%+ referrals.
Built outbound systems for 100+ service businesses. Happy to share what's working if you're open to it."

## Pain, Proof, Ask (Nick Saraev's default)

**Best for:** Overwhelmed founders, burned buyers. Nick's primary framework. Direct, no fluff.

```
Pain: Name the specific pain (their situation, not generic)
Proof: Your credibility in one sentence (specific numbers)
Ask: One clear CTA
```

**Cold email example:**
"Finding consistent, quality leads without burning out is the thing nobody's solved. [Pain]
I've built outbound systems for 100+ service businesses across 10+ countries. Same playbook, AI-powered now. [Proof]
Can I send over a 90-sec video showing how this would work for {company}? [Ask]"

---

## Empathy, Story, Ask

**Best for:** Burned agency buyers specifically. They need to feel understood before they'll trust.

```
Empathy: Show you've been where they are (shared experience)
Story: Brief personal story that builds credibility
Ask: Soft CTA, low commitment
```

**Cold email example:**
"I know what it's like to spend $5K/month on a lead gen agency and have nothing to show for it. [Empathy]
Ran my own agency for a decade. Built 100+ systems. Eventually built one for myself that actually worked. [Story]
Happy to show you what it looks like if you're curious. No pitch, just a walkthrough. [Ask]"

## Framework Selection Guide

| Avatar | Primary framework | Secondary | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| agency_founder | AIDA or BAB | SAS (if signal exists) | They're doing well, show the opportunity |
| overwhelmed_founder | PAS (only with signal) | Pain, Proof, Ask | They feel the pain but need proof |
| burned_agency_buyer | Empathy, Story, Ask | Star, Story, Solution | They need trust before anything |
| growth_mode_founder | AIDA | BAB | Logic and momentum, not pain |
| tech_curious_founder | AIDA | Question Framework | They respond to systems thinking |
| fractional_executive | BAB | Question Framework | Peer-to-peer, not salesy |
| consulting_founder | BAB | AIDA | Professional, outcome-focused |

## Rules for framework usage

1. **SAS overrides everything when a signal exists.** A fresh buying signal + SAS framework beats any website-based framework.
2. **Never use PAS without a real signal.** Assumed pain reads as presumptuous.
3. **BAB is the safest default.** Works for everyone. Never offensive.
4. **Match the framework to the avatar, not the niche.** A burned buyer in CRO needs Empathy, Story, Ask — same as a burned buyer in Creative.
5. **The template body is fixed.** Only the icebreaker + bridge change per framework. The offer paragraph stays the same.
6. **A/B test frameworks, not word-level changes.** Test AIDA vs BAB, not "curious" vs "wondering."
